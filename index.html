Imperialism: Definition & Reason

What Is Imperialism?


“American imperialism consists of policies aimed at extending the political, economic and cultural influence of the United States over areas beyond its boundaries.” - Wikipedia


Reasons for Imperialism

  • Economy: Other nations have profitable goods and resources that can be sold to multiple markets in various countries to improve the American economy
  • Political Competition: With a greater political control, the United States can protect international trades and interests, and establish coaling stations, or navy bases, abroad
  • Military Competition: Gaining, extending, and improving their military power to compete with that of the other countries
  • American Exceptionalism: A belief, central to American political culture since the Revolution, that Americans have a unique mission among nations to spread freedom and democracy
  • Cultural Superiority: The belief that the American culture is superior compared to other cultures
